Here are a few things we know about the Red Fort - it’s the place where the PM hoists the national flag on Independence Day; it was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the mid 17th century…and well, that’s about it! Spread across 256-acres of land.

This fort speaks of the city’s history that not many folks are familiar with. So, in today’s lesson on Delhi Chronicles, we thought of unearthing some interesting yet lesser-known facts about the Red Fort that every Delhiite should know about!

  • It Was Originally White In Colour

  • It Is A World Heritage Site

  • Koh-i-Noor Diamond Belonged To The Fort’s Royal Family

  • The Entrance Of The Fort Is Called Lahori Gate

  • The Rang Mahal Is Situated In The Premises Of The Fort

  • Its Original Name Was Qila-E-Mubarak

  • There Used To Be A Water Gate In The Fort

  • The Fort Is Octagonal-Shaped
